About Me

Background and Interest

I am a passionate researcher and academic focused on developing innovative Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence solutions to tackle complex engineering problems. My work bridges the gap between theoretical research and practical applications, utilizing advanced computational methods to drive insights and solutions.

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Bradford University, where I develop algorithms and computational frameworks at the intersection of machine learning, high-performance computing, and data science. My work focuses on making large-scale scientific simulations faster through the use of hybrid machine learning models.

My Ph.D. at Brandenburg University explores experimental fluid dynamics analysis of the hydrodynamics and optimisation of micro processes involved in pelleting flocculation. I am passionate about bridging the gap between theoretical computational methods and practical real-world impact.

Research Philosophy

A research approach grounded in scientific discovery, interdisciplinary curiosity, and societal impact.

My research philosophy is grounded in the application of scientific discovery to address pressing societal problems, thereby transforming lives and positively impacting communities. I strongly believe that impactful research arises from deep understanding of core principles, combined with a willingness to explore interdisciplinary frontiers.

Collaboration is central to my scientific approach, and I actively seek partnerships across disciplines to amplify the reach and relevance of my research. Whether in developing sustainable process engineering solutions in the water and wastewater sector or investigating complex fluid-particle dynamics and novel transport phenomena models, my research work is primarily driven by curiosity and purpose.

Research Interests

Core areas of focus and expertise

⚗️

Physicochemical Simulation

Investigating systems where heat transfer, mass transfer, fluid motion, and chemical transformation are tightly coupled. The emphasis is on multiscale simulation that connects molecular behavior, reactive transport, and phase dynamics to the design of complex engineering and environmental processes.

🌊

Computational Fluid Dynamics

Applying numerical solutions of the Navier-Stokes equations to understand turbulent, multiphase, and transport-dominated flows in realistic systems. The goal is to turn high-fidelity simulation into practical insight for reactor design, flow control, and performance optimization across challenging fluid environments.

🧠

Scientific Machine Learning

Developing physics-aware machine learning models that embed governing equations, conservation laws, and scientific structure into data-driven prediction. This includes physics-informed neural networks, neural operators, and surrogate models that make simulation faster, more scalable, and more reliable in scientific and engineering settings.
